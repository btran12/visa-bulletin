What is a priority date?

The date USCIS received your immigrant petition — Form I-130 (family) or I-140 (employment). It locks your place in the visa queue.

"C" = Current — no wait

When a category shows "C", all qualified applicants may proceed immediately regardless of priority date. The backlog in that category has cleared.

Final action vs. filing dates

Final action date — when you can receive a visa/green card.
Dates for filing — an earlier date that may let you submit paperwork first, but only when USCIS approves it at uscis.gov/visabulletininfo.

Why do India & China wait so long?

A per-country cap limits each nationality to ~7% of all visas per year (~25,620). India and China have far more applicants than their cap allows — creating decades-long backlogs for EB-2 and EB-3.

Country of chargeability

Visas are charged to your country of birth, not citizenship. If your spouse was born in a different country with a shorter wait, you may be able to use their birth country — this is cross-chargeability.

Retrogression

Dates can move backwards if demand spikes. Always check the latest bulletin before acting. The current bulletin notes retrogression may happen later in FY2026.

EB-5 set-asides

EB-5 reserves 20% for rural investments, 10% for high-unemployment areas, 2% for infrastructure. These are currently "Current" for all countries including China and India.
